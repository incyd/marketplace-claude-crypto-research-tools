### 1.4 Stablecoins as Global Financial Infrastructure

- **Status**: $16T+ annual volume — functioning as global payment rails, not just trading pairs
- **What it is**: Stablecoins powering remittances, payroll, B2B payments, and cross-border commerce
- **Why it matters**: Banks quietly integrating stablecoin systems — bridging crypto and traditional finance at scale
- **Key dynamics**: Regulatory clarity improving (MiCA in EU, US stablecoin bills); Circle IPO; Tether's dominance vs regulated alternatives; yield-bearing stablecoins (Ethena USDe, Ondo USDY) blurring the line between stablecoins and money market products
- **Key projects/protocols**: USDT (Tether), USDC (Circle), PYUSD (PayPal), DAI/USDS (MakerDAO/Sky), Ethena (USDe), Ondo (USDY)
- **Related terms**: stablecoins, payment rails, cross-border payments, yield-bearing stablecoins, stablecoin regulation

### 1.5 Restaking & Shared Security Economies

- **Status**: Maturing — new networks bootstrap trust from shared validator sets rather than building from scratch
- **What it is**: Extending Ethereum's economic security to middleware, data layers, oracles, and rollup infrastructure
- **Why it matters**: Improves capital efficiency — validators earn yield from multiple services simultaneously
- **Key risks**: Slashing contagion, governance complexity, opaque exposure across restaked services
- **Key projects/protocols**: EigenLayer, Symbiotic, Karak, Kelp DAO, Renzo, EtherFi
- **Related terms**: restaking, AVS, shared security, liquid restaking tokens (LRT), operator sets

### 1.6 Modular Blockchain Architecture

- **Status**: Default design pattern — monolithic chains giving way to specialized layers
- **What it is**: Separating execution, settlement, data availability, and consensus into interchangeable modules
- **Why it matters**: Enables faster iteration, app-specific chains, and removes single-chain performance constraints
- **Key projects/protocols**: Celestia (DA), EigenDA, Avail, Dymension (rollup framework), AltLayer
- **Related terms**: modular stack, data availability, rollup-as-a-service, execution layer, settlement layer, DA layer

### 1.7 Layer 2 Scaling & Rollup Ecosystem

- **Status**: Where the real user activity lives — L2s processing majority of Ethereum transactions
- **What it is**: Optimistic and ZK rollups providing cheap execution with Ethereum security guarantees
- **Key dynamics**: L2 fragmentation and interoperability challenges; shared sequencing; L2-to-L2 bridging; Base (Coinbase) emerging as dominant L2 for consumer apps
- **Key projects/protocols**: Arbitrum, Optimism (OP Stack), Base, zkSync, Starknet, Scroll, Linea
- **Related terms**: L2, rollups, optimistic rollups, ZK rollups, sequencer, L2 interoperability, superchain

### 1.8 DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks)

- **Status**: Resurgent — Kaito DePIN narrative mindshare climbed from 0.7% (Oct 2025) → 1.3% (Nov) → dipped to 0.5% (Dec) → recovered to 1.8% (Feb 2026)
- **What it is**: Token-incentivized physical infrastructure: wireless, compute, storage, energy, sensors
- **Why it matters**: One of few narratives where crypto coordinates real-world resource allocation with measurable usage metrics
- **Key distinction**: Projects with real usage metrics (Helium, Render) vs speculative infrastructure plays — the gap is widening
- **Key projects/protocols**: Helium, Render Network, Hivemapper, IoTeX, DIMO, Filecoin
- **Related terms**: DePIN, decentralized compute, decentralized wireless, proof of physical work

### 1.9 On-Chain Identity & Reputation

- **Status**: Growing — privacy-preserving identity via ZK proofs gaining traction as prerequisite for compliant DeFi
- **What it is**: Proving entitlements without revealing identity; programmable, composable reputation across protocols
- **Why it matters**: Unlocks compliant DeFi (whitelists for institutional pools), sybil resistance, under-collateralized lending, and governance weighting
- **Key projects/protocols**: Worldcoin (World ID), Gitcoin Passport, Polygon ID, Galxe, Privado ID
- **Related terms**: ZK identity, soulbound tokens, proof of personhood, on-chain reputation, verifiable credentials

## 2. Fading Narratives

> Topics losing mindshare, user interest, or capital flows. Agent ④ should flag accounts still heavily focused on these as potentially misaligned with where the market conversation is heading.

### 2.1 AI Tokens & On-Chain AI (Hype Layer)

- **Status**: RAPID DECLINE — Kaito AI narrative mindshare dropped from ~32% (Oct 2025) to ~5.9% (Mar 2026)
- **What happened**: The initial "AI + crypto" hype cycle burned through. Most "AI tokens" were branding exercises, not functional AI infrastructure. Market recognized the distinction between genuine AI infrastructure (compute marketplaces, inference networks) and tokens with "AI" in the name
- **What's still real**: Bittensor (decentralized AI training), Render (GPU compute marketplace), and inference networks have legitimate use cases — but the speculative premium on AI-branded tokens has largely evaporated
- **Agent guidance**: Engagement with AI + crypto content should focus on infrastructure/compute angles, not speculative "AI agent token" narratives. Watch for potential re-emergence if genuine AI-chain integration milestones occur
- **Key projects still relevant**: Bittensor, Ritual, Render (compute), Autonolas (agent frameworks)
- **Fading projects**: Most AI memecoins, generic "AI agent" tokens without functional products

### 2.2 Prediction Markets (Crypto-Native Framing)

- **Status**: Shifting away from crypto — prediction markets are becoming mainstream financial products, not crypto-native innovation
- **Kaito mindshare**: Polymarket steady ~1-2% — but the conversation has shifted from "crypto use case" to "sports betting competitor" and "IPO candidate"
- **What happened**: After the 2024 US election success, prediction markets went mainstream. Polymarket is considering an IPO. DraftKings and Robinhood now offer prediction markets. The casino industry is lobbying against them. The narrative moved from "blockchain disruption" to "fintech product category"
- **Why it's fading as a crypto narrative**: The crypto community already knows prediction markets work. The current battles are regulatory (CFTC vs state gambling laws) and commercial (Polymarket vs DraftKings), not technological. Little remaining alpha in covering prediction markets as a web3 narrative
- **Agent guidance**: Avoid heavy engagement with prediction market content unless it ties back to on-chain infrastructure (new prediction market protocols, AMM designs, resolution oracle innovations)

### 2.3 NFT PFP Collections

- **Status**: Fading — floor prices collapsed; culture shifted to utility NFTs and digital identity
- **What happened**: Speculation-driven collectibles market exhausted. Remaining value concentrated in top-tier collections (Punks, BAYC) as cultural artifacts, not investment vehicles

### 2.4 Play-to-Earn Gaming

- **Status**: Fading — unsustainable token models exposed; pivot to "play-and-earn" with drastically lower expectations
- **What happened**: Token emission-based economies collapsed. Surviving projects focus on gameplay quality first, token integration second

### 2.5 Metaverse Land Sales

- **Status**: Fading — virtual real estate speculation cooled significantly
- **What happened**: Enterprise and gaming shifted focus to spatial computing (Apple Vision Pro ecosystem, Meta Quest) rather than token-gated virtual worlds

### 2.6 X-to-Earn (Move, Sleep, etc.)

- **Status**: Fading — most projects failed to sustain user engagement post-incentive
- **What happened**: STEPN-style models proved unsustainable. User retention collapsed once token incentives decreased

### 2.7 "Web3 Social" (Token-Gated)

- **Status**: Evolved — the tokens-for-access model largely failed, but on-chain reputation and identity survived as useful primitives (now tracked under Active Narrative 1.9)

## 4. Infrastructure & Technical Concepts

### 4.1 Smart Contract Platforms

| Platform | Consensus | Key Feature | Ecosystem Stage |
|----------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Ethereum | PoS | Security + composability baseline | Mature — L2-centric roadmap |
| Solana | PoH + PoS | Speed + low fees | Growing — DePIN + consumer apps |
| Cosmos | Tendermint/CometBFT | App-specific chains (IBC) | Modular — interchain focus |
| Avalanche | Snowball | Subnets for enterprise | Enterprise + gaming |
| Near | Nightshade (sharded) | Chain abstraction | AI + account abstraction focus |
| Sui | Narwhal/Bullshark | Object-oriented model | Gaming + consumer |
| Aptos | AptosBFT | Move language | Enterprise + DeFi |

### 4.2 DeFi Primitives

- **AMMs**: Automated market makers (Uniswap, Curve, Balancer) — constant product/sum formulas for on-chain trading
- **Lending/borrowing**: Over-collateralized (Aave, Compound) and under-collateralized (Maple, Goldfinch); Morpho V2 introducing market-set rates
- **Perpetual DEXs**: On-chain derivatives trading (dYdX, GMX, Hyperliquid) — fastest-growing DeFi vertical; Ondo Perps extending to equity perpetuals
- **Yield aggregators**: Auto-compounding vaults (Yearn, Beefy)
- **Liquid staking**: Staking derivatives that maintain liquidity (Lido, Rocket Pool, Jito)

### 4.3 Key Technical Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **TVL** | Total Value Locked — aggregate value deposited in DeFi protocols |
| **MEV** | Maximal Extractable Value — profit from transaction ordering |
| **EIP** | Ethereum Improvement Proposal — standard for protocol changes |
| **Account Abstraction** | Smart contract wallets replacing EOAs — enables gas sponsorship, batched txs, social recovery |
| **ZK Proofs** | Zero-knowledge proofs — verify computation without revealing inputs |
| **Sequencer** | L2 component that orders and batches transactions before posting to L1 |
| **Data Availability** | Ensuring transaction data is accessible for verification (Celestia, EigenDA) |
| **Interoperability** | Cross-chain communication (LayerZero, Wormhole, Axelar, IBC) |
| **Oracle** | Off-chain data feeds for smart contracts (Chainlink, Pyth, Redstone) |
| **Governance** | On-chain voting for protocol changes (Snapshot, Tally, Governor) |
| **DvP** | Delivery-versus-Payment — atomic settlement mechanism used in tokenized asset transfers |
| **Programmable Compliance** | KYC/AML/accreditation checks encoded in smart contracts for institutional access |

## 5. Market & Ecosystem Context

### 5.1 Macro Environment (as of March 2026)

- Total crypto market cap: ~$2.3T
- Bitcoin dominance: ~55%
- Institutional adoption: Moved beyond ETF flows — BlackRock, Apollo, State Street actively building on DeFi rails
- Regulatory: MiCA enforcement in EU; US stablecoin legislation progressing; SEC maintaining securities framework but enabling tokenized equity pilots
- Sentiment: "Infrastructure over speculation" — the dominant meta. Revenue-generating protocols being valued like businesses. The DeFi revenue narrative is the clearest expression of this shift

### 5.2 Capital Flow Patterns

- DeFi: ~$100B in protocols, growing via institutional inflows. Revenue-sharing governance proposals driving token value accrual
- RWA: ~$25.7B on-chain distributed value. Tokenized treasuries stable, tokenized equities and gold growing fastest
- Stablecoins: $16T+ annual volume — functioning as global payment infrastructure
- DePIN: Revenue metrics improving — projects with real usage separating from speculative plays

### 5.3 Wallet & User Trends

- Smart wallets (account abstraction) removing friction for new users
- Wallets evolving into "financial operating systems" — payments, identity, DeFi, governance, social in one interface
- Mobile-first adoption in emerging markets via stablecoins
- Institutional wallets (Fireblocks, Anchorage) becoming critical infrastructure for TradFi-DeFi bridge

### 5.4 DAO & Governance Evolution

- DAOs transitioning from chaotic experimentation to structured governance with legal wrappers
- "Aave Will Win Framework" as template: protocol-level governance proposals that route revenue to stakeholders
- Key trend: institutional investors (Apollo, BlackRock) acquiring governance tokens for strategic influence — changes DAO dynamics fundamentally
- Key challenge: voter apathy and plutocratic tendencies in token-weighted voting
